Encyclopedia of Life
The Enyclopedia of Life produces species pages that represent aggregated knowledge drawn from multiple digital sources. Map of Life was originally supported by an EOL-BioSynC working group grant and Map of Life and EOL have planned partnerships for sharing each other's products. We anticipate EOL displaying range map products we produce, and Map of Life integrating EOL products such as images into our application.
Global Biodiversity Information Facility
The Global Biodiversity Information Facility is an international organization that, as part of its mandate, faciliates the sharing of predominately species point occurrences from a global network of providers. GBIF has partnered with Map of Life to provide ~150 million records of veretebrate occurrences, in order for those records to be mapped and further used in refining species distribution products.
International Union for Conservation of Nature

The International Union for Conservation of Nature develops spatial data products as part of its larger mandate, especially range maps, utilizing experts. These expert opinion range maps are a key data product that are redistributed via Map of Life. IUCN has graciously agreed to partner with us in order to make their maps available for display in Map of Life.

UNEP-WCMC
The World Conservation Monitoring Program provides the World Database of Protected Areas (WDPA). Associated with the WDPA is a biological inventory or checklist of species located in those protected areas, which we serve as another key data source in Map of Life.
Senckenberg Museum and BIK-F
The Senckenberg Gesellschaft für Naturforschung (SGN), the Biodiversität und Klima Forschungszentrum Frankfurt (BIK-F) and the Map of Life (MOL) initiative are united in their mission to produce/document species distribution information, make it available and used for biodiversity and global change research and policy. The BIK-F/SGN partnership, data mobilization and software development support will enable Map of Life to build up a more advanced infrastructure that will directly address the use cases and needs of BIK-F/SGN and similar institutions.
GMBA
MoL and GMBA are partnering in order to explore mountain biodiversity, focusing on global mountain "filters" such as ruggedness, thermal belts, etc. These will be incorporated into Map of Life. The wide range of climatic conditions and topographies across mountains offers an unparalleled opportunity for developing and testing biodiversity theory. Mountains are thus 'experiments by nature' and the results can be explored by e-mining of openly accessible, interconnected electronic databases for scientific biodiversity research.
