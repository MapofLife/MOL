Map of Life
An infrastructure for integrating global species distribution knowledge
VISION

The geography of life on Earth lies at the heart of the interaction between nature and human society with its need for natural resources. As disasters such as hurricanes or oil spills strike or the climate changes, knowledge about the geography of biological diversity – the types of species inhabiting a certain area – is essential for informed and targeted action. Geographic data on species’ distributions are critical for governments, agencies and companies seeking to make sound decisions about land management, health and climate change policies or species conservation. Geographic range maps are displayed in nature programs, zoos, and field guides to the broader public worldwide and have myriad uses from basic science to resource management, development and education. Yet to date the availability and quality of species range maps remains shockingly dispersed and limited – in stark contrast with meter-level resolution remotely sensed environmental information which is accessible globally. The time is now right to mobilize and, most importantly, integrate species distribution information at global scale for scientists and the public. The proposed “Map of Life” project will host all available types of information about species distributions, provide model-based integration, quantify uncertainty in individual and integrated data types, and implement user feedback.

MOL PRODUCTS

MOL will mobilize and integrate all key information about species’ ranges that is currently spread over different research domains: expert opinion maps, surveys over defined extents, point observations (e.g. aggregated by GBIF) and voucher specimens, expert habitat preferences, and others. Combined with high resolution (30-100m) remotely sensed data, a full statistical model-based integration will be performed that allows for different data types to complement each other. The end result will be a knowledge-base of initially tens and ultimately hundreds of thousands of species distribution maps, along with a set of tools for querying, accessing, modifying, downloading and summarizing them. Maps will be easily re-provisioned in other applications such as Encyclopedia of Life (eol.org).
