Encyclopedia of Life
Collaborating partner and funding support from EOL-BioSynC
Map of Life was originally supported by an EOL-BioSynC working group grant and Map of Life and EOL have planned partnerships for sharing each other's products. We anticipate EOL displaying range map products we produce, and Map of Life integrating EOL products such as images into our application.
Global Biodiversity Information Facility
Data sharing partner
The Global Biodiversity Information Facility helps distribute species occurrences records via global network of providers. GBIF has graciouly provided its data for redistribution in Map of Life, in order for those records to be mapped and used in refining species distribution products.
International Union for Conservation of Nature
Data sharing partner
The International Union for Conservation of Nature develops several spatial data products. Their expert opinion range maps are a key data product redistributed via Map of Life.
UNEP-WCMC
Data sharing partner
The World Conservation Monitoring Program provides the World Database of Protected Areas (WDPA) and a biological inventory or checklist of species located in those protected areas, another key data source redistributed in Map of Life.
Senckenberg Museum and BIK-F
Institutional partner
The Senckenberg Gesellschaft für Naturforschung (SGN), the Biodiversität und Klima Forschungszentrum Frankfurt (BIK-F) and Map of Life are united in our mission to produce, document and publish species distribution information. The BIK-F/SGN partnership will enable us to develop a better infrastructure that directly addresses the needs of BIK-F/SGN and similar institutions.
GMBA
Institutional partner
GMBA and Map of Life will explore mountain biodiversity together, focusing on "filters" such as ruggedness and thermal belts. Map of Life will faciliate the e-mining of openly accessible, interconnected electronic databases for scientific biodiversity research.
